What Does an Emergency Locksmith Cost?

Published June 9, 2026

Average Emergency Locksmith Pricing (2025)

Most emergency locksmith calls break down into three cost layers: the service-call fee, labor, and parts or materials. A residential lockout in the middle of the day might cost $75–$125 total. The same lockout at 3 a.m. on a holiday can push $200–$250.

Service-call fee: Covers the locksmith's travel to your location. Typically $50–$100, though many companies waive it when you hire them for the job. Labor: The actual work — unlocking a door, rekeying a cylinder, or replacing hardware. Expect $50–$150 depending on complexity. Parts: A new deadbolt runs $30–$150 retail; locksmiths mark up hardware but install it on the spot.

Automotive emergencies often cost more. Transponder chip-key programming can add $100–$300 on top of the service call because the equipment and blanks are expensive. Older mechanical car keys are cheaper — sometimes under $100 total.

Commercial jobs vary widely. Rekeying a storefront with ANSI Grade 1 deadbolts and a panic bar takes longer and costs more than a simple residential knob. Budget $150–$400+ for commercial emergency calls, especially after hours.

What Drives Emergency Locksmith Costs Higher

Time of day. Night, weekend, and holiday rates exist because techs work outside normal business hours. A midnight lockout can cost 50–100% more than the same job at noon. That's industry standard, not price gouging.

Lock type and security level. A basic pin-tumbler knob unlocks in minutes. A high-security deadbolt with sidebar pins or a restricted keyway takes specialized tools and more time. Smart locks with electronic deadbolts add complexity — some can be reset; others need full replacement if the batteries died and you're locked out.

Damage or forced entry. If a lock is jammed, broken, or someone tried to force it open, the locksmith may need to drill the cylinder and replace the entire lockset. Drilling is a last resort, but it's sometimes the only safe option. Expect parts and labor to climb when replacement is necessary.

Location and travel distance. Urban service calls cost less per mile than rural ones. If you're 30 miles outside a locksmith's normal service area, you'll pay a higher trip charge. Mobile locksmiths price distance into the quote.

Residential Lockout vs. Rekey vs. Lock Replacement

A lockout means you're locked outside and the lock still works — the locksmith picks or bypasses it without damage. Cost: $75–$150 for most homes during business hours, more at night. If you have a spare key inside, some locksmiths can unlock in under five minutes.

Rekeying changes the pins inside the cylinder so old keys no longer work. You get new keys that fit the same hardware. Useful after a breakup, lost keys, or moving into a new home. Rekeying one lock costs $20–$50 in labor; most locksmiths discount additional locks on the same trip. A three-lock rekey averages $80–$120 total.

Replacement means installing entirely new hardware — deadbolt, knob, strike plate, everything. You'd replace if the lock is damaged, outdated, or you want to upgrade security. A basic residential deadbolt replacement runs $100–$200 installed. High-security or smart locks cost $200–$500+ depending on the product.

Emergency situations blur these lines. A jammed lock might need replacement even if you only wanted a lockout. Transparent locksmiths explain options and costs before starting work.

Automotive Locksmith Costs: Mechanical vs. Transponder Keys

Car lockouts split into two price tiers. Mechanical keys (older vehicles, no chip) cost less. The locksmith unlocks the door, cuts a new key from the lock code, and you're done. Expect $75–$150 total for most sedans and trucks.

Transponder and chip keys (most cars since the late 1990s) require programming. The physical key blank is cheap, but the locksmith needs a programmer to sync the chip with your car's immobilizer. Total cost: $150–$350 depending on make and model. European luxury brands and newer push-button fobs can run $300–$500 because blanks and programming are proprietary.

If you're locked out and need a new key, many mobile locksmiths can do both on-site. Dealerships often charge more and require a tow. A 24-hour mobile locksmith saves you the tow bill and gets you back on the road faster.

Trunk lockouts, ignition-cylinder work, and broken-key extractions add labor. A key snapped inside an ignition costs $50–$100 to extract, plus the price of a new key if needed.

How to Avoid Surprise Charges and Get an Honest Quote

Call and describe the problem in detail. Tell the locksmith:

  • Type of lock (deadbolt, knob, car door, smart lock, etc.)
  • Whether the key is lost, broken, or you're just locked out
  • Time of day and your location
  • Any visible damage or previous repair attempts

A reputable locksmith will give you a price range over the phone. It's an estimate — the final cost can shift if the lock is more damaged than expected — but honest companies quote close and explain any changes before they start.

Red flags: Vague "$15 service call" ads that balloon to $300 once the tech arrives. Demands for cash only. No business name, license number, or insurance. If a quote sounds too good, it probably is. Licensed, insured locksmiths cost a bit more up front, but you get legal recourse and quality work.

When to Call an Emergency Locksmith (And When You Might Not Need One)

Call immediately if you're locked out of your home late at night, stranded in a parking lot with no spare key, or facing a broken lock that won't secure your door. A 24-hour mobile locksmith solves these problems on the spot.

You might not need emergency service if you have a spare key elsewhere, a friend or family member with a key, or a landlord who can let you in during business hours. Waiting until morning can save you $50–$100 in after-hours fees. Weigh the cost against your safety and schedule.

If a lock is sticking or a key is hard to turn, don't force it. A quick service call during business hours costs less than an emergency lockout after you snap the key inside the cylinder. Preventive maintenance — lubricating pin-tumbler locks with graphite, tightening strike plates, checking deadbolt alignment — avoids many emergency calls altogether.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does a 24-hour emergency locksmith cost?

Emergency locksmith calls typically range from $75 to $250+ depending on time of day, lock type, and whether you need a simple unlock or full replacement. After-hours and holiday rates are higher due to premium labor costs.

Do locksmiths charge more at night?

Yes. Night, weekend, and holiday rates are standard across the locksmith industry because technicians work outside normal business hours. Expect 50–100% higher labor fees for late-night or holiday service.

What's the difference between a lockout and a rekey?

A lockout means you're locked outside and the locksmith opens the door without damage. A rekey changes the internal pins so old keys no longer work, giving you new keys for the same lock hardware.

Why do car key replacements cost so much?

Modern car keys have transponder chips that must be programmed to your vehicle's immobilizer system. The programming equipment and key blanks are expensive, driving costs to $150–$350 or more for chip keys.

Will a locksmith give me a price before starting work?

Reputable locksmiths provide an estimate over the phone and a final quote on-site before any work begins. Always ask for a written estimate and approve any price changes before the locksmith proceeds.
