Lost Your House Keys? Rekey Instead of Replacing Locks

Published June 11, 2026

What to Do Right Now If You Lost Your Keys

First: breathe. Losing your keys doesn't automatically mean someone will break in, but you need to act fast.

Check your pockets, car, bag, and anywhere you've been in the last few hours. If you're locked out right now, call a licensed mobile locksmith. A professional can get you back inside without damage and rekey your locks on the spot so the lost keys won't work anymore.

Don't hide a spare key under a doormat or fake rock—that's the first place a burglar checks. If you need emergency access, a 24/7 mobile locksmith is safer and faster than breaking a window.

Rekey vs Replace: What's the Difference?

Rekeying means a locksmith changes the pins inside your existing lock cylinder so old keys no longer work. You get new keys, but the lock hardware stays the same. It takes minutes per lock and costs a fraction of full replacement.

Replacing means removing the entire lock and installing a new one. You only need this if the lock is damaged, worn out, or you want to upgrade to a higher ANSI/BHMA grade or a smart lock.

For lost keys, rekeying is almost always the right call. Your deadbolts and knob locks stay in place, you get fresh keys, and your home is secure again—usually within an hour.

When Rekeying Is Enough (Most of the Time)

If your locks are in good shape and you simply lost your keys, rekeying solves the problem. Most pin-tumbler deadbolts and entry knobs can be rekeyed quickly without removal.

Rekeying works for:

  • Standard Kwikset, Schlage, or Yale pin-tumbler locks
  • Deadbolts and keyed knobs on the same keyway (so one new key opens everything)
  • Any lock where the cylinder mechanism isn't damaged or heavily worn

A mobile locksmith brings pin kits and can rekey multiple locks on-site. You'll have new keys in hand before they leave, and the old ones are worthless.

When You Should Replace Instead of Rekey

Replace locks if:

  • The lock is visibly damaged, rusted, or the keyway is worn smooth
  • You're upgrading to a Grade 1 deadbolt (the highest ANSI/BHMA security rating) or a smart lock
  • The lock is a wafer tumbler or low-quality builder-grade hardware—rekeying cheap locks sometimes costs as much as replacing them with better ones
  • You want matching finishes or a unified smart-home system

If you're unsure, a locksmith can assess your hardware in seconds. Many times, even older locks can be rekeyed if the cylinder is solid.

How Much Does Rekeying Cost vs Replacing?

Rekeying typically runs $20–$50 per lock, depending on the lock type and your location. If you rekey three locks, expect $60–$150 total, plus the service call (often waived when you book the job).

Replacing a deadbolt costs $75–$200 per lock, including hardware and labor. High-security or smart locks push that higher.

Rekeying saves you money and time. You skip the trip to the hardware store, avoid mismatched finishes, and your locks are secure in under an hour. For most lost-key situations, it's the clear winner.

Should You Rekey All Your Locks or Just the Front Door?

Rekey every lock the lost keys could open. If your key ring had keys to the front door, back door, and side garage entry, rekey all three.

Don't assume the finder won't know which door the key fits. A motivated intruder will try every entry point. Rekeying only the front door leaves your home vulnerable through the back.

Most locksmiths offer package pricing for multiple locks. Rekeying three to five locks at once is fast and keeps your entire perimeter secure under one new key.

Can You Rekey Locks Yourself?

Technically, yes—rekey kits exist for common brands like Kwikset and Schlage. But unless you're experienced, DIY rekeying is tricky. You need the correct pin sizes, follower tools, and a steady hand. One mistake and the lock won't turn, or worse, you'll jam the cylinder.

If you're locked out, DIY isn't an option anyway—you need inside access to remove the lock. A mobile locksmith can rekey from outside in minutes, no disassembly required.

For a few locks, professional rekeying is cheap, fast, and guaranteed to work. Save yourself the frustration and call a licensed locksmith.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does it take to rekey a lock after losing house keys?

Most locksmiths can rekey a single lock in 10–15 minutes. Rekeying three to five locks typically takes under an hour, including travel time.

Will rekeying work if I lost my only key?

Yes. A locksmith can pick or decode the lock to determine the current pin configuration, then rekey it and cut new keys—even if you have no original key.

Can I rekey my locks to match one key?

Absolutely. If your locks are the same brand and keyway (like all Kwikset or all Schlage), a locksmith can rekey them so one new key opens every lock.

Do I need to rekey if I just moved into a new house?

Yes. You don't know how many copies of the old keys exist. Rekeying ensures previous owners, contractors, or tenants can't access your home.

Is rekeying as secure as replacing the entire lock?

Yes, as long as the lock hardware is in good condition. Rekeying changes the internal pins so old keys won't work—security is identical to a new lock with the same grade.
